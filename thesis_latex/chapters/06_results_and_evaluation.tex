\chapter{Results and Evaluation}
\label{results}

This chapter presents the empirical results of the four experiments defined in \Cref{sec:experiments}. The structure mirrors that of the experiment definitions: the main cross-dataset benchmark is reported first, followed by the dataset scaling experiment with its embedded CPU-versus-GPU break-even analysis, the MLP architecture variation on HIGGS, and finally the temporal carbon intensity counterfactual. All emissions values reported in this chapter use the corrected CO\textsubscript{2} estimate that combines the hardware-measured CPU energy with CodeCarbon's GPU and RAM contributions, as defined in \Cref{implementation_emissions_limitations}. The systematic gap between this corrected value and CodeCarbon's original estimate is reported alongside the main benchmark and discussed in detail in \Cref{discussion}.

\label{results_scaling}

\section{Temporal Carbon Intensity Analysis}
\label{results_carbon_intensity}

The carbon intensity counterfactual analysis is purely retrospective: no models are retrained, and the recorded energy of every existing run is re-multiplied by carbon intensity values drawn from one full year of hourly ElectricityMaps data for the German bidding zone. \Cref{fig:results_intensity_scenarios} reports the eight scenarios defined in \Cref{tab:carbon_scenarios}, \Cref{fig:results_intensity_heatmap} shows the joint hour-of-day and month structure of the German grid, and \Cref{fig:results_scenario_per_run} reports the resulting counterfactual emissions per run.
