\chapter{Results}
\label{results}

This chapter reports the empirical results of all experiments conducted in this study. It covers the main cross-dataset benchmark across all model and dataset combinations, the XGBoost CPU versus GPU comparison, the HIGGS scaling analysis, the MLP architecture variation, the carbon intensity analysis, and the lifecycle break-even analysis. A brief section on measurement validation precedes the benchmark results to establish the reliability of the corrected CO\textsubscript{2} values used throughout.

% ── Measurement Validation ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Emissions Measurement Validation}
\label{sec:results_measurement}

Before presenting the benchmark results it is worth establishing the reliability of the emission values. As described in \Cref{implementation_emissions}, CodeCarbon's TDP-based CPU estimate was replaced by direct hardware-sensor readings. \Cref{fig:codecarbon_vs_hw} shows the ratio between the two for every model-dataset combination.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{codecarbon_vs_hw.pdf}
\caption{CodeCarbon underestimation factor (hardware-corrected\,/\,CodeCarbon) per model and dataset. All values exceed~1, confirming systematic undercounting. Higher factors for Wine and Credit reflect CodeCarbon's TDP-based CPU estimation being least accurate on short training runs; RF has no HIGGS measurement.}
\label{fig:codecarbon_vs_hw}
\end{figure}

% ── Tuning Overhead ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Hyperparameter Tuning Overhead}
\label{sec:results_tuning}

Tuning itself consumes energy that forms part of the total experimental budget. \Cref{tab:tuning_overhead} shows the CO\textsubscript{2} cost and duration of every Optuna study (40 trials each), together with the best weighted F1 achieved.

\begin{table}[htbp]
  \centering
  \caption{Hyperparameter tuning overhead per model and dataset. Each run comprised 40 Optuna
           trials optimising weighted F$_1$. CO$_2$ was measured with the hardware-corrected
           tracker; duration covers the full \texttt{study.optimize} call. Random Forest was
           not tuned on HIGGS; literature defaults were used instead.}
  \label{tab:tuning_overhead}
  \begin{tabular}{llrrr}
    \toprule
    Dataset & Model         & CO$_2$ (g) & Duration   & Best F$_1$ \\
    \midrule
    \multirow{3}{*}{Wine}
      & Random Forest &  0.48 &      77\,s & 0.9831 \\
      & XGBoost       &  1.04 &  2.1\,min  & 0.9888 \\
      & MLP           &  0.39 &      46\,s & 0.9943 \\
    \midrule
    \multirow{3}{*}{Credit}
      & Random Forest & 10.51 & 13.6\,min  & 0.8001 \\
      & XGBoost       &  1.08 &  2.2\,min  & 0.8008 \\
      & MLP           &  6.34 & 15.7\,min  & 0.8044 \\
    \midrule
    \multirow{2}{*}{HIGGS}
      & XGBoost       &  21.9 &   50\,min  & 0.7597 \\
      & \textbf{MLP}  & \textbf{2\,149} & \textbf{95.4\,h} & \textbf{0.7873} \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}
